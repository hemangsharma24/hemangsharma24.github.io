## Overview

Bosch's teams all worked with the same customers, but rarely saw the same view of them — sales, quality, logistics and engineering each held a different piece. CXP brings every order, return, project and escalation into one place, so any Bosch user can pick up a customer thread and respond with the full picture.

---

## The Problem

Bosch operates across multiple B2B divisions serving enterprise customers. A single customer account might have active relationships with several Bosch teams simultaneously — procurement is negotiating a new contract while logistics is handling a delayed shipment while quality is managing an open escalation.

- **Fragmented customer visibility** — no single Bosch employee could see the complete state of a customer relationship without pulling from multiple internal systems
- **Slow escalation response** — when a customer raised an urgent issue, the first step internally was often a round of "what do we even know about this customer?" before addressing the issue
- **Missed cross-sell and account health signals** — patterns visible in aggregate were invisible because no one had a cross-functional view
- **Relationship gaps at handoffs** — account ownership transitions were rough because context lived in the heads of individuals

---

## Research and Approach

The CXP portal had to serve several distinct Bosch user types — account managers who needed relationship depth, logistics coordinators who needed order status at a glance, quality leads who needed escalation history, and executives who needed account health summarised at the portfolio level.

**Discovery:**
- Stakeholder interviews with representatives from 4 Bosch business functions
- Mapping of existing data sources (ERP, CRM, quality management system, project tracker)
- Analysis of how account managers currently assembled customer views — mostly Excel exports and email threads
- Review of existing enterprise CX tools (Salesforce, SAP Customer Experience)

---

## The Design

*Customer profile as the canonical unit:* The portal is organised around the customer, not around Bosch's internal functional divisions. Every entry point leads to a unified customer view that surfaces all relationship dimensions.

*Progressive detail:* The executive summary layer shows account health, open escalations, and recent activity at a glance. Each dimension expands to full detail.

*Cross-functional timeline:* A unified activity feed spanning all functions gives any Bosch user a quick orientation to recent customer activity without needing to know which system to check.

*Role-appropriate actions:* The portal adapts what actions are surfaced based on the logged-in user's function — a logistics coordinator sees ship-and-track actions; an account manager sees relationship actions.

---

## Working with the Business Team

Enterprise B2B design operates at a different tempo than consumer product design. The decision-making cycles are longer, stakeholder alignment takes more time, and the cost of a wrong call in a production enterprise system is high.

The most interesting design problem in this project was the role-adaptive interface: building something that felt coherent to every user type without being a different product for each.

---

## Impact

The CXP portal was adopted by Bosch account teams as the primary interface for customer check-ins ahead of key meetings.

- Cross-functional customer view eliminated the need for pre-meeting email chains to assemble account status
- Escalation response time improved due to faster internal context-building
- Executive portfolio view enabled account health monitoring that wasn't previously possible
