## Overview

A mobile app that walks GINTAA's delivery partners through their entire day — getting set up on the platform, taking on orders, completing deliveries, and seeing what they've earned. I designed almost every part of the experience, shaped by close collaboration with the GINTAA team and the way their operations actually work on the ground.

Available on iOS & Android.

---

## Context & Problem

GINTAA is a food delivery platform operating in India. As the platform scaled, they needed a dedicated delivery partner app that could:

1. **Onboard new partners quickly** — reducing the time from signup to first delivery
2. **Support in-field operations** — real-time order routing, navigation handoff, and status updates
3. **Surface earnings clearly** — daily, weekly, and incentive earnings with enough detail for partners to trust the numbers
4. **Work reliably in variable connectivity** — delivery routes pass through areas with inconsistent data coverage

The existing flow was partially managed through WhatsApp groups and a generic form tool — workable at small scale, but not fit for a growing partner base.

---

## Discovery and Collaboration

The engagement ran as a close collaboration with the GINTAA operations and product teams. Because delivery partner behaviour is hyperlocal, design decisions needed to reflect how GINTAA's network actually worked — not a generic food-delivery mental model.

**Research inputs:**
- Operational brief from GINTAA team covering the end-to-end partner journey
- Review of competitor delivery partner apps (Swiggy, Zomato, Dunzo) for interaction patterns
- Understanding of GINTAA's order management and routing backend

---

## What I Designed

**Platform scope:**
- **Partner mobile app** (iOS + Android): onboarding, availability toggle, order accept/reject, navigation, delivery confirmation, earnings dashboard
- **Admin portal** (web): partner management, order assignment oversight, earnings reconciliation, support queue

**Onboarding:** Built as a document verification + training flow that partners could complete asynchronously on their own device. Progress is saved so incomplete onboarding can be resumed.

**Order flow:** Designed around the physical reality of delivery — the app knows when the partner is at the restaurant, when they've picked up, and when they're near the drop-off. Status transitions are triggered by location with manual override.

**Earnings:** The earnings screen breaks down each component — base delivery fee, distance bonus, surge pay, incentive completions — so partners understand how their total is built.

---

## User Persona

The primary user is a delivery partner — often operating on a two-wheeler, managing the app while navigating unfamiliar streets, frequently in low-connectivity zones. Every interaction was stress-tested against: one-handed use, glance-ability, and what happens when the network drops mid-order.

---

## High Fidelity Designs

The high-fidelity designs were executed across the full partner journey:

- Onboarding & document verification screens
- Home screen with availability toggle and order feed
- Order accept/reject with 10-second window
- In-delivery navigation handoff (Google Maps integration)
- Delivery confirmation with photo capture
- Earnings dashboard with daily / weekly / incentive breakdown
- Admin portal with partner fleet overview

---

## Side Notes

The admin portal was an interesting parallel track: it serves a completely different user (the operations manager sitting at a desk), but the underlying data model is the same. Designing the two in parallel meant decisions made in the partner app had immediate implications for what the admin needed to see.

---

## Working with Engineering

Offline resilience was a joint decision between design and engineering. Core order status updates queue locally and sync when connectivity is restored. Partners see a clear offline indicator so they know the app is recording even when not transmitting.

---

## Outcome

The app shipped on both iOS and Android. The admin portal went live alongside.

- Onboarding completion rate improved compared to the previous multi-channel process
- Order acceptance UI tested positively with pilot group of delivery partners
- Earnings transparency reduced incoming support queries related to payment questions

This was one of the most operationally grounded design projects I've worked on. Food delivery logistics have tight time constraints and zero tolerance for ambiguous UI — a delivery partner can't spend three seconds figuring out a button while on a motorcycle.
