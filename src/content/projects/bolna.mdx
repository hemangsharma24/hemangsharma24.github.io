## Overview

When Bolna.ai needed a new website before their Y Combinator launch, the brief was tight — two weeks, no in-house designers, no spare developer bandwidth. I owned the redesign end-to-end, starting with a sitemap and competitor analysis that shaped the structure before a single screen was drawn.

---

## The Constraint

Two weeks. No in-house designers. No spare engineering bandwidth. High stakes — first impressions from the YC announcement would shape early perception of the product. The constraint wasn't a problem to solve around; it was the design brief.

The response: front-load structure, back-load execution. Spending time upfront on sitemap and competitor analysis sounds counterintuitive under deadline pressure — but it was the decision that prevented expensive late-stage rework.

---

## Starting with the Sitemap, Not the Pixels

**Week 1 — Structure before screens:**

Defined the page hierarchy first: Home, Product, Use Cases, Pricing, Docs (external), Blog (future). The YC announcement context meant the Home page was carrying most of the conversion weight — it needed to work for both developers (who'd want API docs fast) and business decision-makers (who'd want a demo).

Reviewed 6 voice AI and conversational AI product websites. Key observations:
- Most leaned heavily on demo videos — effective but high production cost
- The best performers used interactive samples (play a call, not just watch a video)
- Trust signals mattered early in the page — logos, YC badge, call quality metrics

---

## Process & Collaboration

Low-fidelity wireframes for the 3 core pages reviewed with the full Bolna team. Feedback round resulted in two structural changes: adding a "How it works" section to the Home page, and moving the integration logos section higher.

The structure was agreed by day 5. Week 2 was pure execution — high-fidelity design and handoff.

---

## The Design

Dark base with strong contrast — positions Bolna as a serious technical product, not a consumer app. Large audio waveform motif as a visual language for voice. One demo call embedded prominently above the fold on Home.

A component library was built in Figma to accelerate dev handoff. Worked directly with the developer on spacing tokens and interactive states to reduce back-and-forth during build.

## Outcome

The website shipped on the day of the YC announcement. Zero design-to-dev rework cycles on the final build week. Structure and clarity of the product section was frequently cited by early visitors as a differentiator.

This project was a lesson in the value of systematic work under time pressure. The instinct under a two-week deadline is to skip to screens immediately. Resisting that instinct — spending days 1–3 on sitemap and research — was what made the execution fast.
